    Yesterday, my wife and I dropped by for curbside dine in for Valentines Day. We've been trying to support as many restaurants in the area as possible, and thought we'd drop in to Downtown Pleasanton Main St, which was buzzing. We were encouraged by the restaurant 5 star reviews, and my wife is Italian.  We were seated right away at around 4pm, ordered, & shared their meat lovers pizza and an eggplant parmigiana, & 2 drinks. We both thought the loud rap music playing was distasteful and a turn off, although the generally younger gyrating wait staff seemed to like it.  (Luckily the music changed for the better, near the end of our meal.) We were given bread and their small olive oil balsamic container on plastic Italian looking plates.

    Our entrees arrived rather quickly, but both were luke-warm upon arrival.  Both were tasty enough, however for $24 the eggplant was an extremely small 5" square served on a plastic Italian looking bowl.  It tasted as if it was pre-made and reheated. The luke warm pizza had a good tasty crust, but was otherwise avg at best.  Their menu indicated veggies came with the eggplant, but they never showed up. 15 mins into our meal the waiter returned to check on us, when I mentioned the barely warm entrees, which out of desperation we'd nearly consumed. The younger waiter claimed that "only American eggplant parmesan came with veggies, not Italian." Later, when I held up my phone & pointed out the inclusion of vegetables on their online Valentines Day menu, he danced around it and told me "well, the eggplant is a vegetable!"

    Well, that surprised me.  Ok, not gonna start something over a few veggies. A few minutes later he returned & comped us 2 liqueurs, and we received a bill for $84. While we sipped the liqueurs, the table across from us received 2 piping hot pizzas.  We paid our bill, left a tip and thanked the waiter upon departure.  Very unfortunately, plastic plates and all, we won't be back, so be advised.

I began with TRIS DI MONTANARE - The chefs selection of three small deep-fried pizzas that were about 3 inches wide and 3 inches tall. Each of them delectable. Imagine a crispy dough piled with wonderful Burrata cheese and perhaps Crudo salami or a tomato sauce sprinkled with grated cheese.  It was tempting to cut them with a knife and fork but the waiter said they are meant to be eaten by hand and so....a little messy ... but they were wonderful.!
